Risk Management
Calculation Methodology
Understanding the math behind every number this calculator produces.
// Core Formula
Position Size = Risk Capital ÷ Risk Per Unit

// Where:
Risk Capital   = Fund Available × (Risk% ÷ 100)
Risk Per Unit  = Buy Price − Stop Loss Price
Investment    = Position Size × Buy Price
R:R Ratio     = (Target − Buy) ÷ (Buy − Stop Loss)
Net P&L       = (Shares × Reward Per Unit) − (Brokerage × 2)

📏 Fixed Fractional Method

This calculator uses the Fixed Fractional Position Sizing method — the gold standard used by professional traders worldwide. You risk a fixed percentage of your total capital on every trade, regardless of trade size or stock price. Losses compound slowly, gains compound fast.

🎯 Why Stop Loss Matters

Stop loss price is the backbone of position sizing. The tighter your stop loss, the more shares you can buy for the same risk amount. Conversely, a wide stop means fewer shares. This forces traders to be precise about entry and SL levels before placing any order.

⚖ Risk:Reward Psychology

A minimum 2:1 R:R ratio means for every ₹1 risked, you aim to make ₹2. Even winning only 40% of trades with a 2:1 R:R keeps you profitable. R:R below 1:1 means one loss wipes out more than one win — a losing proposition long-term.

💰 Brokerage Impact

Most traders ignore brokerage on small positions, but on high-volume intraday trades it eats profits significantly. Net P&L accounts for brokerage on both the buy leg and sell leg, giving you a realistic profit/loss figure.

Getting Started
How to Use the Calculator
Five simple steps. Takes less than 30 seconds per trade.
1

Enter Your Total Trading Capital

Input the total amount of money available for trading — your overall portfolio capital. The calculator determines how much to deploy based on your risk tolerance.

💡 Use your actual trading account balance, not total savings.
2

Set Your Risk Percentage

Decide what percentage of total capital you're willing to lose on this one trade. Beginners: start at 1%. Experienced traders: 1–2%. Never exceed 3–5%.

⚠ Keep this consistent across all trades for disciplined risk management.
3

Enter Buy Price & Stop Loss

Input your planned entry price and the price at which you will exit with a loss. The gap between these two numbers determines your risk per share. Set your stop loss before entering the trade.

💡 Use technical levels like support zones or previous lows for stop loss placement.
4

Set Target Price (Recommended)

Enter your profit target to instantly calculate the Risk:Reward ratio. Aim for minimum 2:1 — only take trades where potential reward is at least double the risk.

🎯 Green R:R (≥2:1) = good setup. Red = skip the trade.
5

Read Results & Execute

Instantly see shares to buy, capital deployed, maximum risk, expected profit, and net P&L after brokerage. Hit Copy Results to paste into your trade journal, Notion, or WhatsApp.

📋 Always journal your trades — use Copy Results to paste directly.
